### 🎬 文案 2：移动平均线交叉策略

```

We're looking at Apple's daily chart with two moving averages: the 50-day in blue and the 200-day in orange.

Here's the key insight: these crossovers work best on higher timeframes like the daily or weekly chart. On lower timeframes, you'll get too many false signals.

### 🎬 文案 3：RSI 超买超卖策略

```
RSI, or Relative Strength Index, is one of the most popular momentum indicators. And today, I'll show you how to read it like a pro.

RSI measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a scale from 0 to 100. The magic numbers are 30 and 70.

When RSI drops BELOW 30, the asset is considered OVERSOLD. This often means selling pressure is exhausted and a bounce could be coming. Smart traders look for buy setups here.

When RSI rises ABOVE 70, the asset is OVERBOUGHT. Buying momentum may be fading, and a pullback could be near. This is where you consider taking profits or selling.

But here's what most beginners miss: RSI can stay overbought or oversold for extended periods during strong trends. So NEVER trade RSI alone. Always combine it with price action at support and resistance levels.

The real edge comes from RSI DIVERGENCE — when price makes a new high but RSI makes a lower high. That's a powerful reversal signal.

### 🎬 文案 4：如何设置止损止盈

```
Most traders lose money not because they're wrong about direction, but because they don't manage risk properly. Today, I'll teach you how to set stop losses and take profits like a professional.

Rule number one: ALWAYS set your stop loss BEFORE you enter a trade, not after. This removes emotion from the decision.

For a long position, place your stop loss just BELOW the nearest support level. Not right at it — give it some breathing room. If support is at $100, maybe set your stop at $97.

For your take profit, target the next resistance level. The key ratio to remember is 1 to 3. If you're risking $3 on your stop loss, you should aim for at least $9 on your take profit. This is called a favorable risk-reward ratio.

Even if you're only right 40% of the time, with a 1:3 risk-reward ratio, you'll still be profitable over the long run. That's the power of risk management.

Remember: protecting your capital is always more important than chasing profits. A small loss is just the cost of doing business.

Thank you for watching! Follow me for more professional trading tips. What's your risk-reward ratio? Share it in the comments!

### 🎬 文案 5：趋势线交易入门

```
Trend lines are the most visual and intuitive tool in technical analysis. If you can draw a straight line, you can trade trends.

To draw an UPTREND line, connect at least two higher lows with a straight line. Price should bounce off this line multiple times. The more touches, the more valid the trend line.

For a DOWNTREND line, connect at least two lower highs. Each time price touches this line and drops, it confirms the trend is still in control.

The real trading opportunity comes when a trend line BREAKS. A trend line break is often the first signal that the trend is changing direction. But wait — you need a CONFIRMED break. Price must close beyond the trend line, not just poke through it temporarily.

Pro tip: After a trend line breaks, the old trend line often flips from support to resistance, or vice versa. This is one of the most reliable trading setups you'll find.

Thank you for watching! If you learned something new, hit that follow button. Have your own trend line tips? Drop them in the comments!
