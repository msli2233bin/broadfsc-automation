**Key Concepts Explained Simply**

### Fundamental Analysis

* **Definition**: Fundamental analysis is a method of evaluating an investment's value by analyzing its underlying economic and financial conditions.
* **Tools**: Fundamental analysis uses various tools, such as financial statements, ratios, and metrics, to determine a company's value.
* **Types**: There are two types of fundamental analysis:
	+ **Qualitative analysis**: Examines harder-to-measure factors, such as management quality and brand strength.
	+ **Quantitative analysis**: Analyzes easily measured factors, such as earnings and assets.

### Financial Statements

* **Balance Sheet**: A snapshot of a company's financial position at a specific point in time.
* **Profit and Loss Statement**: A record of a company's revenues and expenses over a specific period.
* **Financial statements** contain key metrics, such as profitability, assets, and growth rates.

### Key Metrics

* **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**: The ratio of a company's stock price to its earnings per share.
* **Strength, Weakness, Opportunity, and Threat (SWOT) Analysis**: A qualitative analysis method for analyzing a company's competitive position.

### Trading Strategies

* **Value Investing**: A strategy that involves buying undervalued companies with strong fundamentals.
* **Comparative Analysis**: A strategy that involves comparing a company's financial metrics to those of its peers.

### Market Insights

* **Economic Moat**: A sustainable competitive advantage that provides a company with a long-term edge over its competitors.
* **Macro Environment**: The overall economic and market conditions that affect a company's performance.

* **How to Read and Analyze an Income Statement**: A step-by-step guide to understanding a company's financial performance.
* **Using the Price-to-Earnings Ratio and PEG to Assess a Stock**: A guide to using the P/E ratio as a metric for evaluating a company's value.
* **Qualitative Analysis: What Makes a Company Great?**: A guide to understanding the qualitative factors that contribute to a company's success.

### Practical Trading Tips

* **Compare P/E ratios to similar companies in similar sectors**: To get a better understanding of a company's value.
* **Use SWOT analysis to identify opportunities and threats**: To inform investment decisions.
* **Analyze financial statements to determine a company's profitability and growth rates**: To evaluate a company's financial health.

### Key Terms

* **SEC Form S-1**: A registration form filed by companies seeking to become publicly traded in the U.S.
* **SEC Form 10-K**: An annual financial report required by the SEC for all publicly traded companies in the U.S.
* **Porter's 5 Forces**: A framework for analyzing a company's competitive position.
